The
Forgotten
World

In the age before maps, there was a world that all other worlds dreamed of — vast and impossible and humming with a quiet kind of power that had no name yet.

Then came the cartographers, and with their lines and borders, something immeasurable was lost. Not destroyed. Not erased. Simply… forgotten.

One scholar. One impossible map. One chance to remember what the world has chosen to leave behind.

III · About

Prashmit Mohapatra

Prashmit Mohapatra writes at the intersection of history, mythology, and the deep human need to name what cannot be named. His work explores the spaces between maps — the territories that exist just beyond the edge of the known world.

Drawn to questions of memory, cartography, and the stories that survive in the silence between records, Prashmit spent years tracing the borders of forgotten places before setting them to page.

The Forgotten World is his debut — a novel that begins where all good maps end.
